10th Standard Mathematics

Unit Test Model Paper (Chapters 1-5)

Time Allowed: 1 Hour Maximum Marks: 50

Part - I (10 x 1 = 10 Marks)

Note: Answer all the questions.

  1. If $f(x) = x^2 - 1$, then $f(f(x))$ is:

  2. If the HCF of 65 and 117 is expressible in the form $65m - 117$, then the value of $m$ is:

  3. If the first term of an A.P is 20 and the common difference is -2, then the 10th term is:

  4. The solution of $(2x-1)^2 = 9$ is equal to:

  5. The number of points of intersection of the quadratic polynomial $x^2 + 4x + 4$ with the X-axis is:

  6. In $\triangle LMN$, $\angle L=60^\circ, \angle M=50^\circ$. If $\triangle LMN \sim \triangle PQR$ then the value of $\angle R$ is:

  7. The area of a triangle with vertices $(0,0), (4,0)$ and $(0,5)$ is:

  8. The slope of the line joining $(2, -1)$ and $(4, 3)$ is:

  9. The range of the relation $R = \{(x, x^2) | x \text{ is a prime number less than 13}\}$ is:

  10. If $A$ is a $2 \times 3$ matrix and $B$ is a $3 \times 4$ matrix, how many columns does $AB$ have?

Part - II (5 x 2 = 10 Marks)

Note: Answer any 5 questions.

  1. A function $f$ is defined by $f(x)=3-2x$. Find $x$ such that $f(x^2)=(f(x))^2$.

  2. Find the 19th term of an A.P. $-11, -15, -19, \dots$

  3. Find the LCM of $p^2-3p+2$ and $p^2-4$.

  4. In $\triangle ABC$, $D$ and $E$ are points on the sides $AB$ and $AC$ respectively such that $DE \parallel BC$. If $AD = 8x-7$, $DB=5x-3$, $AE=4x-3$ and $EC=3x-1$, find the value of $x$.

  5. Show that the points $P(-1.5, 3), Q(6, -2), R(-3, 4)$ are collinear.

  6. Find the sum of the series $1+2+3+ \dots +60$.

  7. Form a quadratic equation whose roots are $7 + \sqrt{3}$ and $7 - \sqrt{3}$.

Part - III (4 x 5 = 20 Marks)

Note: Answer any 4 questions. Question No. 25 is compulsory.

  1. Let $f(x) = x-4, g(x) = x^2$ and $h(x) = 3x-5$. Show that $(f \circ g) \circ h = f \circ (g \circ h)$.

  2. Find the sum of all natural numbers between 300 and 600 which are divisible by 7.

  3. Find the square root of $36x^4 - 60x^3 + 61x^2 - 30x + 9$.

  4. State and prove the Basic Proportionality Theorem (Thales' Theorem).

  5. Find the area of the quadrilateral formed by the points $(8,6), (5,11), (-5,12)$ and $(-4,3)$.

  6. Find the sum of the series $10^3 + 11^3 + \dots + 20^3$.

  7. (Compulsory) The average age of Vani, her father, and her grandfather is 53. One-half of her grandfather's age plus one-third of her father's age plus one-fourth of Vani's age is 65. Four years ago, if Vani's grandfather was four times as old as Vani, then how old are they all now?

Part - IV (1 x 10 = 10 Marks)

Note: Answer any one of the following.

  1. Construct a triangle similar to a given triangle PQR with its sides equal to $\frac{4}{5}$ of the corresponding sides of the triangle PQR (scale factor $\frac{4}{5} < 1$).

    (OR)

    Draw the graph of $y = x^2 - 4x + 4$ and state the nature of its roots.

II (Marks: 50)

Note: Answer any 10 questions. Question No. 42 is compulsory.

  1. Let $f: A \to B$ be a function defined by $f(x) = \frac{x}{2} - 1$, where $A = \{2,4,6,10,12\}$ and $B = \{0,1,2,4,5,9\}$. Represent $f$ by (i) a set of ordered pairs, (ii) a table, (iii) an arrow diagram, and (iv) a graph.

  2. The sum of three consecutive terms that are in an A.P. is 27 and their product is 288. Find the three terms.

  3. Find the square root of the expression: $4x^4 - 12x^3 + 37x^2 - 42x + 9$.

  4. State and prove the Angle Bisector Theorem.

  5. Find the equation of a straight line passing through the point of intersection of the lines $8x+3y=18$ and $4x+5y=9$ and bisecting the line segment joining the points $(5, -4)$ and $(-7, 6)$.

  6. From the top of a tower 50 m high, the angles of depression of the top and bottom of a tree are observed to be 30° and 45° respectively. Find the height of the tree.

  7. (Compulsory) Find the sum of the series $10^3 + 11^3 + \dots + 20^3$.

Part - IV (Marks: 16)

Note: Answer both questions.

  1. Construct a triangle similar to a given triangle PQR with its sides equal to $\frac{7}{4}$ of the corresponding sides of the triangle PQR (scale factor $\frac{7}{4} > 1$).

    (OR)

    Draw a circle of radius 4.5 cm. Take a point on the circle. Draw the tangent at that point using the alternate segment theorem.

  2. Draw the graph of $y = x^2 + x - 2$ and hence solve $x^2 + x - 2 = 0$.

    (OR)

    Draw the graph of $y = x^2 - 4x + 4$ and state the nature of its roots.
