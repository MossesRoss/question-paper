10th Standard Mathematics

Quarterly Examination - Model Paper

Time Allowed: 3 Hours Maximum Marks: 100

Instructions: Check the question paper for fairness of printing. If there is any lack of fairness, inform the Hall Supervisor immediately. Use Blue or Black ink to write and underline and pencil to draw diagrams.

Part - I (Marks: 14)

Note: (i) Answer all the 14 questions. (ii) Choose the most suitable answer from the given four alternatives.

  1. If $f(x) = 2x^2 + 1$, then the value of $f(x+1) - f(x)$ is:

    (A) $4x+2$ (B) $4x-2$ (C) $2x+4$ (D) $2x-4$

  2. The first term of an A.P. is 5 and its 10th term is 41. Find the common difference.

    (A) 3 (B) 4 (C) 5 (D) 2

  3. The solution of the system $x + y - 3z = -6, -7y + 7z = 7, 3z = 9$ is:

    (A) $x=1, y=2, z=3$ (B) $x=-1, y=2, z=3$ (C) $x=-1, y=-2, z=3$ (D) $x=1, y=-2, z=3$

  4. If $\triangle ABC$ is similar to $\triangle DEF$ such that $BC = 3$ cm, $EF = 4$ cm and area of $\triangle ABC = 54$ cm$^2$. The area of $\triangle DEF$ is:

    (A) 72 cm$^2$ (B) 96 cm$^2$ (C) 108 cm$^2$ (D) 64 cm$^2$

  5. The slope of the line which is perpendicular to a line joining the points $(0,0)$ and $(-8,8)$ is:

    (A) -1 (B) 1 (C) $1/3$ (D) -8

  6. The value of $\sin^2\theta + \frac{1}{1+\tan^2\theta}$ is equal to:

    (A) $\tan^2\theta$ (B) 1 (C) $\cot^2\theta$ (D) 0

Part - II (Marks: 20)

Note: Answer any 10 questions. Question No. 28 is compulsory.

  1. A relation $R$ is given by the set $\{(x,y) | y = x + 3, x \in \{0,1,2,3,4,5\}\}$. Determine its domain and range.

  2. Find the sum of the first 15 terms of the A.P. $8, 7\frac{1}{4}, 6\frac{1}{2}, 5\frac{3}{4}, \dots$

  3. Find the GCD of the polynomials $x^3+x^2-x-1$ and $x^3+3x^2+3x+1$.

  4. State and prove the converse of Thales' Theorem.

  5. Find the area of the quadrilateral whose vertices are at $(-9, -2), (-8, -4), (2, 2)$ and $(1, -3)$.

  6. Prove the identity: $\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}} = \sec\theta + \tan\theta$.

  7. (Compulsory) If $A = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}$ and $B = \begin{pmatrix} 5 & 6 \\ 7 & 8 \end{pmatrix}$, find $AB$.

Part - III (Marks: 50)

Note: Answer any 10 questions. Question No. 42 is compulsory.

  1. Let $f: A \to B$ be a function defined by $f(x) = \frac{x}{2} - 1$, where $A = \{2,4,6,10,12\}$ and $B = \{0,1,2,4,5,9\}$. Represent $f$ by (i) a set of ordered pairs, (ii) a table, (iii) an arrow diagram, and (iv) a graph.

  2. The sum of three consecutive terms that are in an A.P. is 27 and their product is 288. Find the three terms.

  3. Find the square root of the expression: $4x^4 - 12x^3 + 37x^2 - 42x + 9$.

  4. State and prove the Angle Bisector Theorem.

  5. Find the equation of a straight line passing through the point of intersection of the lines $8x+3y=18$ and $4x+5y=9$ and bisecting the line segment joining the points $(5, -4)$ and $(-7, 6)$.

  6. From the top of a tower 50 m high, the angles of depression of the top and bottom of a tree are observed to be 30° and 45° respectively. Find the height of the tree.

  7. (Compulsory) Find the sum of the series $10^3 + 11^3 + \dots + 20^3$.

Part - IV (Marks: 16)

Note: Answer both questions.

  1. Construct a triangle similar to a given triangle PQR with its sides equal to $\frac{7}{4}$ of the corresponding sides of the triangle PQR (scale factor $\frac{7}{4} > 1$).

    (OR)

    Draw a circle of radius 4.5 cm. Take a point on the circle. Draw the tangent at that point using the alternate segment theorem.

  2. Draw the graph of $y = x^2 + x - 2$ and hence solve $x^2 + x - 2 = 0$.

    (OR)

    Draw the graph of $y = x^2 - 4x + 4$ and state the nature of its roots.
